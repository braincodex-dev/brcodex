Methodological Integrity

Prove­nance

Every sentence written here carries a verifiable anchor in biology or logic. This page is that anchor made visible — the epistemic chain, the verification filters, the lexicon of precision.

“If in any thing I have differed from that which is commonly received, it was with a purpose to proceed in melius — better — not in aliud — merely different. The intention was amendment and proficiency, not change and divergence.”

Francis Bacon · De Augmentis Scientiarum

I

The Epistemic
Chain of Custody

Brain Codex operates exclusively from primary matter: original research articles, raw sequencing datasets, canonical philosophical texts. Manuals, review-of-reviews, and market-grade summaries that dilute the argument for commercial accessibility are refused as epistemic input. The chain of custody runs from source to sentence, with no intermediate laundering.

The selection of what enters this corpus obeys no metric of institutional impact, no departmental affiliation, and no coalition that trades transdisciplinary scope for the comfort of a single citation index. The curatorial independence is not a preference — it is a structural condition of the project's validity.

II

Three
Verification Filters

Before any claim earns the status of a verb inside Brain Codex — a statement with operative force, not decorative weight — it must survive three orthogonal filters. A claim that clears only two is an hypothesis, appropriately marked. One that fails all three is noise, and noise is not published.

Filter 01 — Molecular
The Proof of Matter
Does the fact resist biophysics and molecular neurobiology? The claim must be consistent with what is known at the level of ion channels, receptor kinetics, signal transduction cascades, and genomic architecture. Phenomenological elegance does not override mechanistic constraint.
Filter 02 — Historical-Philosophical
The Proof of Time
Does the concept possess intellectual lineage, or is it a terminological hallucination of modernity? Every construct used in this corpus must be traceable to a tradition of rigorous use — not merely to a recent paper that adopted the word for rhetorical convenience.
Filter 03 — Phenomenological
The Proof of Being
Does the theory survive contact with the first-person structure of conscious experience? Following Merleau-Ponty, no account of cognition that cannot be reconciled with the irreducible fact of embodied, situated experience passes this filter.

Provenance is the coordinate where all three vectors converge. When a claim carries a provenance marker, it declares alignment with all three filters. The absence of that marker is itself information.

III

Lexicon of
Precision

The contemporary academic idiom has developed a remarkable tolerance for semantic drift: terms migrate from their technical contexts into general circulation, return wearing new clothes, and are received as insight rather than recognized as echo. Brain Codex maintains a controlled vocabulary against this entropy.

Terms such as consciousness, neuropsychopharmacology, fractal dynamics, and epigenetic modulation are used in the strict sense established by the primary literature and the philosophical tradition. Where a term is used in a restricted or technical sense that diverges from common usage, the divergence is explicitly declared. Where a label offers convenience at the cost of precision — populist psychometric categories, commercially inflected diagnostic groupings, ethnographic generalisations dressed as biology — the label is refused and the underlying structure described in adequate technical language instead.

The Lexicon of Precision is under active construction. A dedicated page will enumerate the terms, their operational definitions within this corpus, and the sources that anchor each definition. Status: forthcoming.

IV

Permanent
Registry

Provenance must be auditable. The following infrastructure supports open verification of Brain Codex content:

Indexing & Persistent Identifiers — Works are registered with DOI allocation via Zenodo and supplementary deposit on SSRN where applicable. The author's ISNI (0000-0005-3022-7377) provides persistent identity resolution across indexing systems including CrossRef and DataCite.

Open Neuroscience — Where dataset provenance is relevant to an argument, integration with the Canadian Open Neuroscience Platform (CONP) and allied repositories is the preferred mode of citation, in preference to secondary data summaries.

The Scriptorium — Editorial production under the Brain Codex imprint treats typographic and material form as an extension of intellectual seriousness, not decoration. The physical artifact is itself a provenance document: paper weight, typeface selection, and layout grammar are deliberate choices that encode the register of the argument.

The Permanent Registry index is under construction. DOI and preprint links will be surfaced here as individual works are registered. Status: forthcoming.

Brain Codex does not seek consensus; it seeks precision. The provenance declared on this page is the commitment that every sentence carries a verifiable anchor in biology or logic, free from the constraints of institutional coalitions that privilege form over substance. Where that anchor cannot be demonstrated, the claim is not published — it is held, interrogated, and either verified or discarded. The chain of custody does not begin here. It begins at the primary source. This page is merely the declaration that the chain exists and that its integrity is the project's non-negotiable condition.
